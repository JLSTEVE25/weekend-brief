Sunday Brunch at 300 East

Sunday cools down to the low 70s — ideal for sitting on the porch of that charming Victorian house in Dilworth. The vanilla custard French toast is supposedly incredible, and it's family-run with a real kids-welcome vibe. Good chance to rally the Blackburns or Breedens for a laid-back morning.
Dilworth $$ ✓ Kids Friendly Brunch

Pickleball + Tacos at Tipsy Pickle

Date Night

Saturday Night at Supperland

With half of Charlotte packed into BofA Stadium for Morgan Wallen, Plaza Midwood will be blissfully quiet. That restored church, the wood-fired steaks, the speakeasy bar downstairs — this is the move. Book early; the 8:30 slot should work perfectly if you want to skip the concert traffic entirely.

Rada — If You Want Something Special

NY Times and Michelin both noticed this place, and for good reason. The Dover sole is the kind of dish you think about for days, and the curated wine list is genuinely fun to explore. Myers Park on a warm Saturday night, away from the Uptown chaos — it's an easy yes.
Myers Park $$$ Adults Only NY Times + Michelin
Sunday

Sunday Brunch at The Crunkleton

Known for some of the best cocktails in Charlotte, their brunch is a sleeper hit. The Charlotte Hot Brown and biscuits & gravy are the play, and Sunday's cooler temps (72°) make Elizabeth a perfect stroll-and-linger neighborhood. Maybe rally the Blackburns or another couple for a proper Sunday situation.
Elizabeth $$ Adults Only Cocktails + Brunch
Date Night

Mi Cariño — New & Worth Exploring

Opened in January and already generating buzz — 200+ agave spirits and scratch small plates in South End. If you want something new that isn't a full fine-dining commitment, this mezcalería is a great Saturday pre-dinner drinks stop or a full evening on its own. The vibe is warm, moody, and very date night.
South End $$ Adults Only Mezcalería
