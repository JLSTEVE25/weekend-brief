🎶 Tonight

Morgan Wallen — Still the Problem Tour

Bank of America Stadium, 7:00 PM Saturday. It's going to be 90° and packed — hydrate, wear something breathable, and grab dinner beforehand. This is the hottest country ticket of the spring.

Curated for you
Saturday

🍺 Biergarten Day at OMB

Ninety degrees calls for a cold beer and a massive shaded biergarten. Let the boys loose on the playground while you post up with a Copper or a Meck Lager. Pretzels and bratwurst for the table — nobody's cooking in this heat.
South Charlotte $$ ✓ Kid Friendly Go-To
Family

🏓 Pickleball + Tacos at Tipsy Pickle

Camp North End spot with pickleball courts, golf sims, and an arcade — basically engineered for families with young boys burning off energy. Tex-Mex menu is solid, and it's all indoors if 90° feels like too much. Text the Blackburns or the Breedens — their kids would love this.
Camp North End $$ ✓ Kid Friendly Want to Try
Sunday

🥞 Sunday Brunch at 300 East

This charming Victorian home in Dilworth does one of the best brunches in Charlotte — the vanilla custard French toast is the move. Family-run, kids welcome, and Sunday should cool down to 79° so the walk around the neighborhood after is actually pleasant. A real Sunday morning vibe.
Dilworth $$ ✓ Kid Friendly Want to Try
Saturday

🍔 Harriet's Hamburgers — Charlotte's Best

Before or after Morgan Wallen, swing by Optimist Hall for Charlotte Observer's best burger. Black Angus smash patties from a walk-up window — fast, no-fuss, and the boys will demolish them. Pair it with Dumpling Lady or Bird Pizzeria in the same food hall for variety.
Optimist Hall $ ✓ Kid Friendly Best Burger 🏆
Date Night

🥩 Saturday Night at Supperland

If you're not going to Morgan Wallen, take advantage of a babysitter and the warm evening at Supperland. The wood-fired steakhouse in the restored church is still one of the most atmospheric spots in Charlotte — and that speakeasy bar downstairs afterward? Chef's kiss. Michelin Exceptional Cocktails for a reason.
Plaza Midwood $$$ Adults Only Go-To 🏆
Date Night

🍷 Rada — The One the NY Times Found

Basque-leaning, Michelin-noticed, and tucked into Myers Park. The dover sole and lamb bolognese are outstanding, and the wine list is curated without being pretentious. This is the kind of place you walk out of feeling like you actually went out. Book for Saturday — you won't regret it.
Myers Park $$$ Adults Only NY Times + Michelin 🏆
Sunday

🥂 Boozy Brunch at The Crunkleton

Sunday's cooler temps are practically begging for The Crunkleton's Charlotte Hot Brown and a proper cocktail. It's a no-kids zone so this is more of a "send John with the boys to Eddie's and you go here with a friend" situation. The biscuits & gravy are legit.
Elizabeth $$ Adults Only Want to Try
Date Night

🌵 Mi Cariño — Mezcal & Small Plates

Opened January and already one of the hottest spots in South End. Over 200 agave spirits, scratch small plates, and a moody vibe that feels like you stumbled into Mexico City. Perfect warm-weather Saturday pre-game or nightcap after the concert. Grab the Blackburns for a double date.
South End $$ Adults Only New — Jan 2026
