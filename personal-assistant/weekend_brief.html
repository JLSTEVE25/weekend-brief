Rainy Sunday Plan: Tipsy Pickle

Sunday's looking wet (72° with drizzle rolling in), so pivot indoors. Camp North End's Tipsy Pickle has pickleball, golf sims, and an arcade — basically a rainy-day salvation with two young boys. Tex-Mex menu keeps everyone fed. Text the Blackburns or the Millers and make it a group thing. The kids will be occupied for hours.

Sunday Brunch: 300 East

If the drizzle holds off long enough for brunch, 300 East in Dilworth is a family-run gem in a gorgeous Victorian home. The vanilla custard French toast is the kind of thing you'll be thinking about by Tuesday. Kids are welcome, vibe is charming without being fussy. Book ahead — they fill up on rainy Sundays when everyone has the same idea.

Date Night Saturday

Pre-Concert Dinner: Supperland

If you're heading to Morgan Wallen, start the night right at Supperland in Plaza Midwood. The wood-fired steakhouse in that restored church is still one of the coolest rooms in Charlotte, and the speakeasy bar downstairs is perfect for a quick cocktail before heading to the stadium. You're 15 minutes from Bank of America — time it right and you'll feel like actual adults for a few hours.

Date Night Saturday

Skip the Concert: Rada Instead

Not feeling the stadium scene? Use that sitter for a proper date night at Rada in Myers Park. The NY Times and Michelin both noticed this place for good reason — the Dover sole is pristine, the lamb bolognese is rich without being heavy, and the wine list is curated by someone who clearly cares. It's the kind of quiet, unhurried dinner you two don't get enough of.

Rainy Sunday Brunch: Dogwood

Sunday's drizzly and cooler — perfect excuse for a lazy brunch. Dogwood in North End does a Fried Chicken Benny, Hangover Hash, and a free biscuit bar that's borderline irresponsible. They have live music on Sundays, which gives the whole thing an easygoing vibe. Could go either way — take the boys or make it a brunch date while grandparents take the morning shift.

New Spot: The Story of Mi Cariño

Opened in January and already buzzing — this South End mezcalería has 200+ agave spirits and scratch small plates that punch above their weight. It's a great pre-dinner cocktail stop or a full evening if you're in a grazing mood. The vibe is warm and intimate, exactly the kind of place that feels like a discovery. Put this one on the short list before everyone catches on.
